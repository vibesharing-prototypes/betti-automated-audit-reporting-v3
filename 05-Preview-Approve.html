Cybersecurity Risk — Situational Overview
Scope: SEC Peer Incident Response  •  Created on: Feb 24, 2026  •  Audience: Audit Committee
Executive Summary

GlobalTech Financial Services faces material cybersecurity control exposure across three domains that require immediate Audit Committee attention ahead of the March board meeting. This report covers access management control failures, critical vendor cyber risk, and SEC disclosure readiness gaps — assessed against the backdrop of recent enforcement actions against peer institutions.

Audit Scope

The audit covered the following AI systems and processes in scope for this review:

  • Automated risk scoring model (v2.4.1) deployed in the GRC platform
  • Document classification and extraction pipeline (NLP-based)
  • Compliance monitoring alert system and escalation workflows
  • Vendor risk assessment AI assistants — 3 active third-party integrations

Systems excluded from scope: customer-facing product features, experimental models in staging, and AI components managed exclusively by third-party vendors under separate audit arrangements.

Recommendations

Management is recommended to prioritise the following remediation actions in order of risk:

  • Establish a formal AI governance policy with defined roles, model risk tiers, and review cadence
  • Implement an explainability layer for all Tier 1 production models, with decision rationale logged per output
  • Update data retention schedules to comply with applicable privacy regulation across all active jurisdictions
  • Define and enforce bias and fairness testing as a mandatory gate in the model deployment pipeline
  • Configure human review checkpoints for all AI-initiated escalations rated Critical or High priority
